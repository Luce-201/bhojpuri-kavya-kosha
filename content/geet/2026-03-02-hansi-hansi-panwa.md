notes: >-
  कजरी नजरिया से खेलई रे बदरिया  

  कि बरसई रकतवा के नीर  
    
  The first line would roughly translate to "the cloud plays with the kajal of
  my eyes", i.e. by not raining it makes me cry (since if it won't the crop
  would go bad) and when i will cry the kajal in my eyes will be smudged or flow
  out with the tears, the second line makes it even more impactful, i don't shed
  tears but rather blood ("khoon ke aansoo rona" is a proverb used often to show
  the vastness of one's anguish).


  These two lines tell the tale of how monsoon(the rainy season) betrayed them
  by not raining.


  सरदी के मारे छायी  

  जर्दी चनरमा पे  

  गड़ाड़ी मिलि रे तकदीर


  Due to the cold, a shade(referring to fog) has taken over the moon, and
  likewise our fate has gone in a bit of haze. The moon across many folk
  cultures is considered and looked at as the last companion or someone whom
  you'd talk to only if there's no one else (an example of this would be the
  song "chan kithan" where the girl is addressing the moon as his beloved). But
  here the moon too has gone a bit away.


  चढ़ते फगुनवा सगुनवा मनावे गोरी  

  चैता करे रे उपवास


  On the advent of phagun, we start to get hopeful and chaitra leaves us with
  fasting.


  Phagun is celebrated with great excitement because the grains are almost ready
  to be harvested and that's what is indicated in the lyric as well but chaitra,
  the month when harvesting actually takes place, the amount of grain obtained
  is so little (or the grain left after giving the taxes to the British) is so
  little that we have to fast.


  गरमी बेसरमी ना बेनिया डोलाये माने  

  डरे सैयाँ गरवा में फाँस


  The shameless summer doesn't go away no matter how much we fan. and my husband
  puts a rope around his neck(to hang).


  The personification of Summer as shameless is such a treat, someone who is not
  swayed easily even after outright rejection would be considered shameless just
  as that the heat/summer is so bad that no matter how much we fan it doesn't
  seem to leave(the fanning doesn't have any measurable affect on the heat).


  In the end the husband too can't bear this any longer and gives up, leaving
  her all alone.
